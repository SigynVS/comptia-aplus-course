Mobile Devices

Laptop and mobile hardware: installing and configuring components, display parts, ports and accessories, mobile network connectivity, and keeping data in sync across devices.

1.1 Laptop hardware & components

Laptops squeeze desktop functionality into a small, power-efficient package. Components are smaller, often proprietary, and frequently shared with the display assembly. Know how to replace these field-replaceable parts.

Storage & memory

  • SSD (Solid-State Drive) — no moving parts, fast, shock-resistant. Forms: 2.5" SATA, M.2 (gumstick), and mSATA (older).
  • M.2 — a slot/form factor that can carry SATA or NVMe drives. NVMe uses the PCIe bus and is much faster than SATA.
  • SODIMM (Small Outline DIMM) — the laptop RAM form factor. Smaller than desktop DIMMs; installed at an angle and clipped down.

Input & internal devices

  • Keyboard, trackpad, and sometimes a battery — all common field replacements.
  • Migrating/replacing components: power down, disconnect AC, and remove the battery when possible before service.
On the exam: Know the difference between M.2 SATA and M.2 NVMe — same-looking slot, very different speed. NVMe = PCIe lanes.

1.3 Ports & accessories

  • USB-C — reversible connector; can carry data, video (DisplayPort Alt Mode), and power delivery.
  • Thunderbolt — high-speed (uses USB-C connector on TB3/4); video + data + daisy-chaining.
  • Docking station — full desktop connectivity (multiple monitors, Ethernet, USB) via one connection.
  • Port replicator — like a dock but simpler; just multiplies the laptop's existing ports.

Locks & security

A cable/Kensington lock tethers a laptop to a desk to deter theft.

1.4 Mobile network connectivity & synchronization

Cellular standards

  • GSM — uses a removable SIM; globally common.
  • CDMA — older standard, historically tied to the carrier (no swappable SIM in classic form).
  • 4G LTE and 5G — successive generations; 5G adds far greater speed/lower latency.
  • PRL (Preferred Roaming List) — updates which towers a device connects to.
  • IMEI identifies the device; IMSI identifies the subscriber (on the SIM).

Short-range & configuration

  • Bluetooth pairing: enable, make discoverable, pair, enter/confirm PIN, test.
  • NFC — very short range; tap-to-pay and quick pairing.
  • Hotspot / tethering — share a phone's cellular data with other devices.
  • Airplane mode — disables all radios at once.

Email & account setup

ProtocolPortNotes
POP3110 / 995 (SSL)Downloads mail to one device; classically removes it from server.
IMAP143 / 993 (SSL)Syncs mail across devices; mail stays on server.
SMTP25 / 587 / 465Sending mail.
On the exam: If a user wants mail synced across phone, tablet, and laptop, choose IMAP, not POP3.

Synchronization

  • Sync to the cloud (iCloud, Google) vs. sync to the desktop (iTunes/Finder) vs. sync to automobile (CarPlay/Android Auto).
  • Synced data: contacts, calendar, mail, photos, documents, bookmarks, passwords.
  • Requirements: a connection, an account, and adequate storage on both ends.
