Operational Procedures

The "professional" domain: documentation, change management, backups, safety, environmental controls, privacy/licensing, incident response, and communication.

4.1 Documentation & best practices

  • Ticketing systems: clear problem description, categories, severity, escalation, progress notes, and resolution.
  • Asset management: inventory lists, asset tags/IDs, procurement life cycle, warranty, database.
  • Policy docs: AUP (acceptable use), network topology diagrams, SOPs, compliance, knowledge base/articles, regulatory/incident reports.
  • Splash screens, password policy, new-user / end-user device on/offboarding.

4.2 Change management

  1. Documented business processes & a change request with purpose/scope.
  2. Risk analysis and risk level.
  3. Plan for change, end-user acceptance, and a backout/rollback plan.
  4. Change Board approval, sandbox testing, then implement and document.
On the exam: Every change needs a rollback (backout) plan and approval before implementation. Never "just make the change."

4.3 Backups & recovery

FullEverythingOne set; slowest backup.

SyntheticServer-built full from prior backupsLike a full.
  • 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite. Test restores regularly.
  • On-site vs off-site; backup verification & rotation (e.g., grandfather-father-son).

4.4 Safety

  • ESD protection: anti-static wrist strap & mat, equipment grounding, store parts in anti-static bags. (Don't wear a strap when working on CRTs/PSUs with high voltage.)
  • Electrical safety: unplug before service; high-voltage devices (CRT monitors, power supplies, laser printer fusers) hold dangerous charge — don't open.
  • PPE: safety goggles, air filter mask; proper lifting (bend knees); fire safety; trip hazards.
  • Compliance with government regulations for safe handling.
On the exam: An ESD wrist strap protects components from static — but never wear one when servicing high-voltage equipment like a CRT or PSU.

4.5 Environmental controls

  • MSDS / SDS — safety data sheets describe handling/disposal of chemicals (e.g., toner, batteries).
  • Proper disposal: batteries, toner, CRTs, and other devices per local regulation/recycling.
  • Temperature/humidity control, ventilation, dust, and protection from power surges/brownouts with a UPS & surge suppressor.

4.6 Privacy, licensing & policy

  • PII / PHI, GDPR, PCI DSS — handle regulated data carefully.
  • Licensing: personal vs enterprise/corporate, open-source vs commercial, EULA, valid licenses, DRM.
  • Chain of custody and incident response: identify, report through proper channels, preserve data/evidence, document.

4.7 Communication & professionalism

  • Use clear language (avoid jargon/acronyms/slang), maintain a positive attitude, actively listen, don't interrupt.
  • Be culturally sensitive, on time, and avoid distractions (personal calls, texting).
  • Deal with difficult customers: don't argue, don't be dismissive, don't take it personally; set/meet expectations, offer options, follow up.
  • Maintain confidentiality of customer materials located on/around a workstation.

Remote access tools

  • RDP, VPN, SSH, screen-sharing/RMM, third-party tools — note security implications & consent.
  • Basic scripting awareness: .bat, .ps1 (PowerShell), .sh, .py, .vbs, .js — and risks of running untrusted scripts.
