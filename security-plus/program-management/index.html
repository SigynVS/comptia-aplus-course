Security+ · SY0-701 · Domain 5.0 (20%)

Security Program Management & Oversight

The governance layer: policies, risk, third-party management, compliance, audits, and awareness.

5.1 Governance

  • Guidance hierarchy: policies → standards → procedures → guidelines.
  • Policy examples: AUP, information security, BCP/DRP, incident response, SDLC, change management.
  • Structures: boards/committees, roles — owner, controller, processor, custodian, steward.
  • Regulatory/legal/industry drivers; governance, risk, and compliance (GRC).

5.2 Risk management

ConceptNotes
Risk identification & assessmentQualitative vs quantitative.
SLE / ARO / ALEALE = SLE × ARO (annualized loss expectancy).
Risk responsesAccept, avoid, transfer, mitigate (and exemptions/exceptions).
Risk registerTracks risks, owners, likelihood, impact, KRIs.
BIABusiness Impact Analysis → RTO/RPO, MTD.
On the exam: ALE = SLE × ARO. Buying insurance = transfer; shutting down a risky service = avoid.

5.3 Third-party risk

  • Vendor assessment, due diligence, SLA, MOU/MOA, MSA, BPA, NDA, SOW.
  • Supply-chain risk, vendor monitoring, right-to-audit clauses, EOL/EOSL considerations.

5.4 Compliance & audits

  • Frameworks/regs: PCI DSS, HIPAA, GDPR, SOX, ISO 27001, NIST CSF.
  • Internal vs external audits, attestation, penetration testing (known/partially known/unknown environment), reconnaissance (active/passive).
  • Privacy: data subject rights, controller vs processor, data retention, RTBF.
  • Consequences of non-compliance: fines, sanctions, reputational damage, loss of license.

5.5 Security awareness

  • Phishing simulations, anti-social-engineering training, secure behavior.
  • Policy/handbook acknowledgment, reporting suspicious activity, role-based training, recurring campaigns.
Tip: People are the most-targeted attack surface — awareness training is a primary, high-ROI control.
