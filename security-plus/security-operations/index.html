Security+ · SY0-701 · Domain 4.0 (28%)

Security Operations

The biggest domain: hardening, monitoring, identity & access, incident response, and forensics.

4.1 Hardening & secure baselines

  • Establish, deploy, and maintain secure baselines; disable unused services/ports.
  • Harden endpoints, servers, mobile, IoT, network devices; FDE, host firewall, EDR.
  • Patch/vulnerability management: scanning, CVE/CVSS, remediation, rescanning, exceptions.

4.2 Monitoring & tooling

ToolUse

SOARAutomate & orchestrate response (playbooks).
IDS/IPS, antivirus/EDRDetect/block malicious activity.
DLP, NetFlow, vuln scannersExfil prevention, traffic, weakness discovery.
Threat intel feedsIoCs, TTPs (MITRE ATT&CK).

4.3 Identity & access management (IAM)

  • Provisioning/deprovisioning, SSO, federation (SAML, OAuth, OIDC), LDAP.
  • MFA factors: know / have / are / location / behavior; passwordless, FIDO2.
  • Access models: RBAC, ABAC, DAC, MAC, rule-based; PAM for privileged accounts.
  • Concepts: least privilege, separation of duties, just-in-time access, password policies/vaulting.
On the exam: MAC = labels enforced by the system (most rigid); DAC = owner decides; RBAC = by job role; ABAC = by attributes/policy.

4.4 Incident response

  1. Preparation → Detection & analysis → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Lessons learned.
  • Training, testing (tabletop, simulations), playbooks, threat hunting, root-cause analysis.

4.5 Digital forensics

  • Chain of custody, legal hold, order of volatility (collect most volatile first: CPU/RAM → disk → backups).
  • Acquisition: imaging, write blockers, hashing for integrity; e-discovery, preservation, reporting.
Tip: Follow the order of volatility — capture RAM/cache before powering down, since volatile data vanishes on shutdown.
