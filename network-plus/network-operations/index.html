Network+ · N10-009 · Domain 3.0 (19%)

Network Operations

Keeping the network documented, monitored, available, and recoverable.

3.1 Documentation

  • Physical & logical diagrams, rack diagrams, IDF/MDF layouts, cable maps.
  • Baselines, IP address management (IPAM), asset inventory, service-level docs.
  • Policies: AUP, BYOD, NDA, password policy, SLA/MOU.

3.2 Monitoring & metrics

Tool/ConceptUse
SNMPPolls/manages devices (161); traps (162) push alerts.
SyslogCentralized log collection.
Flow data (NetFlow)Traffic analysis by conversation.
SIEMAggregates & correlates logs/alerts.
Performance metricsBandwidth, latency, jitter, packet loss, uptime.

3.3 High availability & disaster recovery

  • Redundancy: NIC teaming, redundant power/links, FHRP, load balancing, clustering.
  • Metrics: RTO (recovery time objective), RPO (recovery point objective), MTTR, MTBF.
  • Sites: hot (ready now), warm (partial), cold (bare facility).
  • Backups: full/incremental/differential, snapshots, the 3-2-1 rule.
On the exam: RTO = how fast you must be back up; RPO = how much data loss is acceptable (how far back the last good backup is).

3.4 Organizational processes

  • Change management with approvals & rollback; configuration management & version control.
  • Lifecycle (EOL/EOSL), patching, vendor/SLA management.
Tip: A warm site is the middle ground — some equipment/data staged, faster than cold, cheaper than hot.
